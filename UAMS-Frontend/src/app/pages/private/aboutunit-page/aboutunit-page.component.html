Unit History

After the independence of Bangladesh, a unit called “X” was established at Shaheed Mostafa Kamal Line Barrack in 1974 under the Army Headquarters DCS to provide communication facilities to the Army. It was later renamed as “Y” in 1976. At present this unit is under “Z” Independent Signal Brigade. Since its inception, this unit has been providing communication from Army Headquarters to all Division Headquarters and all Independent Brigade Headquarters.

In 1989, after a tragic accident at the Tongi Pubail railway station near the Majukhan Bridge, the soldiers of the unit engaged in winter training at a nearby place rushed 116 passengers to the hospital and recovered 55 dead bodies. The efficient and timely action of the battalion saved many precious lives and saved the goods from being looted.
When the Dhaka-Chittagong telephone communication was completely cut off during the catastrophic cyclone in 1991, the members of this unit worked tirelessly day and night to restore the Dhaka-Chittagong communication. From 2007 to 2008, the unit participated in the creation of voter ID cards with photographs in the area for which the unit is responsible. The “Y” battalion was able to carry out the South Asia Games broadcasting on the TV channel from 29 January 2010 to 9 February 2010 accurately and efficiently. During 2020, the Army's Operation Plan provides for the provision of relief supplies, masks, hand sanitizers, and financial assistance to the poorest civilians in its own management, including patrolling the area of responsibility for the Global Epidemic Corona. This unit performs the overall responsibility of “Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujib dhaka marathon 2021” organized on the occasion of Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman's Homecoming Day on 10-1-2020.
A satellite station has recently been built under the supervision of the 11 Signal Battalion and this is a landmark step in modernizing the Army's communications system. This hub station is capable of providing voice, data and video teleconferencing facilities to the formation headquarters through nine terminal stations from Sena Sadar with the help of Bangabandhu Satellite 1. With the installation of this system, Bangladesh Army has entered the satellite era. The Satellite Hub Station was inaugurated by the Hon'ble Chief of Army Staff on 23 February 2021.

Since its inception, the unit has successfully participated in various competitions at the formation level, winning four times in inter-unit assault course competition, four times champion in bayonet fighting competition, three times champion in football competition and 24 times champion in basketball competition.
